## 2. ITEM A2 — MCT FUNCTIONAL READING OF "组织" VERB (Art. 2)

### 2.1 What training corpus contains
🟡 **Plausible-but-not-verified**: Several pre-2023 commentaries (Chinese tourism-law academic literature, 文旅 enforcement summaries) treat the Art. 2 trigger verbs **functionally** — i.e., look at who economically structures the booking, not who is named on the service contract. The signals supporting this:

1. **2018–2019 enforcement against unlicensed "野马团" (rogue tour) WeChat groups** ✅: enforcement targeted organizers who collected money and grouped tourists, even when no formal travel-agency holding-out occurred. This supports the functional reading.

2. **2017 typical-case (典型案例) bulletins**: MCT (then 国家旅游局) periodically published enforcement digests treating "组织" broadly. 🟡 cannot cite specific case numbers from corpus.

3. **2020 在线旅游经营服务管理暂行规定** ✅ implicitly endorses a functional reading by separately regulating "平台经营者" — i.e., MCT distinguishes platforms from travel agencies for **conduct** purposes but does not exempt platforms from **licensing** if their activity is substantively travel-agency business.

## 3. ITEM A3 — 旅行社条例 ART. 48 PENALTY RANGE

**Legal's assumption:** confiscation of illegal gains + RMB 10,000–30,000 (basic); RMB 30,000–100,000 + business license revocation (serious). 🔴

### 3.1 What training corpus contains
✅ **High confidence on order of magnitude**: 旅行社条例 (State Council Decree No. 550) Art. 48 prescribes confiscation of illegal gains and a fine in the **tens-of-thousands RMB range** for unlicensed travel-agency business, escalating for serious cases.

🟡 **Specific numbers**: My corpus recollection for the exact bands is:
- Basic: confiscation + **RMB 10,000–30,000** fine
- Serious / refusal to cease: **RMB 30,000–100,000** fine + revocation of business license

These align with Legal's memo. **However, the 2016 and 2020 amendments to 旅行社条例 may have adjusted these figures upward** — I cannot confirm 🔴.

✅ **Parallel statute** — 旅游法 Art. 95: criminal escalation possible if circumstances are "严重" (serious). Personal liability for 法定代表人 and 直接负责的主管人员 is provided for under both the 旅行社条例 and 旅游法.

### 3.2 Additional penalty exposures not in Legal memo
🟡 The platform should also consider:
- **电子商务法 Art. 80–83**: platform-operator penalties for failure to verify in-platform operator qualifications (e.g., failing to check 导游证) — fines up to **RMB 2 million** in serious cases.
- **市场监管局 (SAMR)** parallel exposure under 反不正当竞争法 / 广告法.
- **PIPL Art. 66**: data-violation fines up to **RMB 50 million or 5% of prior-year turnover**, plus personal liability of RMB 100K–1M for responsible individuals. (Out of scope for this licensing memo but worth signaling.)

### 3.3 Net assessment
Legal's penalty figures are **likely correct as a floor** but may understate composite regulatory exposure (tourism-law penalty + e-commerce-law penalty + potential SAMR penalty + potential personal liability). A consolidated penalty matrix is worth building once counsel confirms current figures.
