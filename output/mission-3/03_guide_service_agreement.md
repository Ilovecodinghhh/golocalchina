## ⚠️ Drafting Notes for Counsel (delete before publication)

1. **Core legal risk this draft is engineered to avoid:** Re-characterization of the Guide as a *de facto* employee (事实劳动关系) under PRC labor law, which would trigger:
   - Social insurance obligations (五险一金) under 《社会保险法》
   - Severance liability under 《劳动合同法》Articles 46–47
   - Working-hours and minimum-wage liability
   - Joint tax liability and back-payment of individual income tax withholding
   See **Schedule A — Independent Contractor Safeguards** at the end of this document for the specific factor-by-factor defense.

2. **Jurisdictional scope:** This draft assumes the Guide is a PRC-resident natural person holding a valid 导游证 issued under 《旅游法》 and 《导游人员管理条例》. For foreign guides or guides operating outside PRC, separate agreement required. [COUNSEL REVIEW]

3. **Platform position throughout:** Information service provider (信息服务提供者), NOT travel agency (旅行社), NOT employer (用人单位), NOT labor dispatch agency (劳务派遣单位). Every clause must be read against this baseline.

4. **Tax characterization:** Guide income from the platform is treated as 劳务报酬所得 (service remuneration) under 《个人所得税法》. Platform's withholding obligations under 《个人所得税法实施条例》 require separate operational SOP. [COUNSEL REVIEW — confirm whether platform withholds at source or guide self-reports.]

5. **Cross-references:** Where this Agreement refers to Privacy Policy or Terms of Service, those documents must be finalized and version-locked before this Agreement is countersigned with any Guide.

## RECITALS

**WHEREAS,** the Platform operates an online information service platform branded "Ctrip-Guides" (the "**Ctrip-Guides Platform**") that displays the profiles, service offerings, availability, and self-set pricing of independent licensed tour guides for the reference of foreign tourists visiting the People's Republic of China;

**WHEREAS,** the Platform is **not a travel agency** (非旅行社), does not organize, sell, or operate package tours (包价旅游产品), and does not employ, supervise, schedule, direct, or manage tour guides in their professional work;

**WHEREAS,** the Guide is an **independent professional service provider** (独立服务提供者) holding a valid 导游证 issued by competent PRC cultural and tourism authorities under 《旅游法》, who freely and at their own discretion decides what services to offer, at what price, and on what dates;

**WHEREAS,** the Guide wishes to use the Platform's matching technology, payment facilitation, and translation tools to reach foreign tourist customers, and the Platform wishes to provide such information services to the Guide in exchange for a transparent information service fee;

**WHEREAS,** the Parties expressly intend to establish a **commercial cooperation relationship** (商业合作关系) of an information-service-provider-and-independent-service-provider character, and **expressly disclaim any intent to form an employment relationship** (劳动关系), agency relationship (代理关系), partnership (合伙), or labor dispatch arrangement (劳务派遣);

**NOW, THEREFORE,** in consideration of the mutual covenants below, the Parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE 1. DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1 **"Booking"** means a confirmed service request placed by a Tourist through the Platform that the Guide has expressly accepted.

1.2 **"Completed Service"** means a Booking for which the Guide has delivered the agreed tour guide service and which has not been cancelled, refunded, or the subject of an unresolved chargeback or complaint.

1.3 **"Guide License"** means the 导游证 issued to the Guide under 《旅游法》 and 《导游人员管理条例》, valid and in good standing.

1.4 **"Information Service Fee"** means the 12% fee defined in Article 5.

1.5 **"Service Fee"** means the gross price set by the Guide and paid by the Tourist for the tour guide service.

1.6 **"Tourist"** means a registered end-user of the Platform seeking tour guide services.

1.7 Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings given in the Platform's Terms of Service or Privacy Policy.

## ARTICLE 2. NATURE OF THE RELATIONSHIP
### (Independent Contractor — Core Provision)

2.1 **Independent service provider status.** The Guide enters into this Agreement as an independent professional service provider. The Guide is **not** an employee (员工), staff member, agent, partner, joint venturer, franchisee, or labor-dispatched worker of the Platform. Nothing in this Agreement creates, and the Parties expressly disavow:
   (a) an employment relationship (劳动关系) under 《劳动法》 or 《劳动合同法》;
   (b) a de facto employment relationship (事实劳动关系);
   (c) any obligation of the Platform to pay social insurance (五险) or housing fund (一金) contributions for the Guide;
   (d) any obligation of the Platform to pay minimum wage, overtime, severance, or any other employment-related compensation;
   (e) any agency relationship under which the Platform acts as the Guide's agent or *vice versa*;
   (f) a travel agency–tour guide relationship under 《旅行社条例》.

2.2 **No supervision or direction.** The Platform does not:
   (a) set the Guide's working hours;
   (b) require the Guide to accept any particular Booking;
   (c) prescribe the route, content, methodology, or manner of delivering the Guide's services;
   (d) require the Guide to wear a uniform, carry Platform-branded equipment, or identify as a Platform employee;
   (e) discipline, evaluate performance for promotion, or otherwise manage the Guide's professional conduct beyond Platform Rules necessary to maintain marketplace quality and Tourist safety.

2.3 **Guide autonomy.** The Guide retains full and exclusive control over:
   (a) **Pricing** — the Guide sets all Service Fees;
   (b) **Availability** — the Guide chooses which dates, hours, and locations to make available;
   (c) **Acceptance** — the Guide may accept or decline any Booking request for any lawful reason;
   (d) **Service content** — the Guide designs the itinerary, narrative, language of delivery, and scope of service in agreement with the Tourist;
   (e) **Method** — the Guide uses their own professional judgment, tools, and methodology.

2.4 **No exclusivity.** The Guide is **free to list, market, and provide services on competing platforms** (including but not limited to Ctrip, Klook, GetYourGuide, Viator, Airbnb Experiences) and to accept direct private clients. The Platform will not impose any non-compete, exclusivity, or "most-favored platform" restriction on the Guide.

2.5 **Guide bears own business costs.** The Guide is solely responsible for:
   (a) own income tax (劳务报酬所得 individual income tax) and, where applicable, VAT (增值税) and surcharges;
   (b) own social insurance (灵活就业社保) and commercial insurance;
   (c) own equipment, transport to the meeting point, professional development, license renewal fees, and continuing education;
   (d) own business registration if required by the Guide's local regulator for freelance income.

2.6 **No tools of employment.** The Platform does not provide the Guide with: a workspace, uniform, employee ID, company email address, performance review system, paid leave, sick leave, or any other indicia of employment.

2.7 **Construction.** Any ambiguity in this Agreement shall be construed **in favor of the independent contractor characterization** stated in this Article 2.

## ARTICLE 3. GUIDE OBLIGATIONS

3.1 **License and qualifications.** The Guide warrants and continuously represents that:
   (a) the Guide holds a valid, current, and unsuspended 导游证;
   (b) the Guide is legally permitted to provide tour guide services in the regions for which they offer services on the Platform;
   (c) the Guide will promptly (within 5 business days) notify the Platform of any suspension, revocation, expiration, complaint by a tourism regulator, or administrative penalty affecting the Guide License;
   (d) the Guide will provide accurate identity, license, and contact information and will keep it updated.

3.2 **Professional conduct.** The Guide will:
   (a) comply with 《旅游法》, 《导游人员管理条例》, and all applicable tourism, safety, cultural heritage, and consumer-protection laws;
   (b) not engage in forced shopping (强迫购物), unauthorized itinerary changes for kickbacks (擅自变更行程), or any other practice prohibited under 《旅游法》 Articles 35, 41, 98–102;
   (c) treat Tourists with professional courtesy regardless of nationality, religion, race, gender, or other protected characteristics;
   (d) maintain reasonable Tourist safety standards;
   (e) honor the terms (price, dates, scope) communicated to the Tourist through the Platform once a Booking is accepted.

3.3 **Insurance.** The Guide shall maintain, at the Guide's own expense, professional liability insurance and personal accident insurance reasonably appropriate for tour guide services. **The Platform does not insure the Guide or the Tourist for tour-incident liability.** [COUNSEL REVIEW — consider whether minimum coverage amounts should be specified, balancing accessibility for small guides versus risk exposure.]

3.4 **Tax compliance.** The Guide is solely responsible for declaring and paying their own taxes on income earned through the Platform. The Guide acknowledges that:
   (a) the Platform may be required to withhold individual income tax at source as a payer of 劳务报酬所得 under 《个人所得税法》;
   (b) the Platform may be required to report Guide payments to tax authorities;
   (c) the Guide will provide accurate tax-related identifying information (e.g., 身份证号, 纳税人识别号 where applicable) and will indemnify the Platform for tax penalties resulting from inaccurate Guide-supplied information.

3.5 **No misrepresentation.** The Guide will not:
   (a) hold themselves out as an employee, agent, or representative of the Platform;
   (b) make legally binding statements on behalf of the Platform;
   (c) collect payments outside the Platform for Bookings originated through the Platform (see Article 5.6);
   (d) make false or misleading statements about the Guide's qualifications, experience, ratings, or licensing.

3.6 **Platform Rules.** The Guide will comply with the Platform's published rules of operation (community standards, listing requirements, dispute-resolution policy), which are limited to the minimum necessary to operate a safe, lawful marketplace and **do not constitute employment supervision**.

## ARTICLE 5. FEES AND PAYMENT

5.1 **Information Service Fee.** In consideration of the information, matching, payment-facilitation, and translation services provided, the Platform shall charge an **Information Service Fee (信息服务费) equal to 12% of the gross Service Fee** of each Completed Service. The Information Service Fee is the Platform's sole compensation under this Agreement.

5.2 **Payment flow.** The Tourist pays the gross Service Fee to the licensed PSP (Airwallex), which holds the funds pending Completed Service. Upon Completion (as defined in the Platform's Terms of Service, including the relevant Tourist confirmation or auto-confirmation window), the PSP disburses:
   (a) 88% of the gross Service Fee to the Guide's nominated PRC bank account or compliant payment account; and
   (b) 12% Information Service Fee to the Platform.

5.3 **Settlement timing.** Standard settlement is **T+3 business days** from Completion, subject to PSP processing times, anti-money-laundering checks, and applicable holds for disputed Bookings.

5.4 **Tax withholding.** Where the Platform is required by PRC law to withhold individual income tax (个人所得税) on the Guide's 劳务报酬所得 before remitting payment, it will do so and provide the Guide with a withholding receipt. [COUNSEL REVIEW + Tax — operational SOP needed.]

5.5 **Refunds and chargebacks.** If a Tourist receives a refund (full or partial) under the Platform's cancellation policy, or initiates a successful chargeback or regulatory complaint requiring refund:
   (a) the corresponding share of the Service Fee is reversed from the Guide; and
   (b) the corresponding portion of the Information Service Fee is reversed from the Platform, **except** where the refund results from Guide misconduct (no-show, license invalidity, gross breach of Article 3), in which case the Platform may retain the Information Service Fee as compensation for matching costs already incurred. [COUNSEL REVIEW — fairness/consumer-law sensitivity.]

5.6 **No off-platform payment for Platform-originated Bookings.** Where a Tourist–Guide match is originated through the Platform, the Guide shall not solicit or accept payment outside the Platform for the same engagement or for an obvious extension of that engagement booked on the same trip. **For independent direct-client relationships not originated through the Platform, the Guide is entirely free to transact off-platform** — this restriction applies only to a circumvention of Platform-originated matches and shall not be construed as exclusivity.

5.7 **Disputes about fees.** Fee disputes shall first be raised through the Platform's in-app support channel; unresolved disputes proceed under Article 11.

## ARTICLE 7. CANCELLATION AND NO-SHOW

7.1 **Guide-set cancellation terms.** The Guide sets their own cancellation policy from a menu of standard policies offered by the Platform (e.g., Flexible / Moderate / Strict). The Platform facilitates application of the policy the Guide has selected; **the Platform does not unilaterally impose cancellation terms on the Guide.**

7.2 **Tourist cancellation.** When a Tourist cancels, the Platform applies the Guide's selected cancellation policy automatically and disburses refunds and Guide compensation accordingly.

7.3 **Guide cancellation.** If the Guide cancels a confirmed Booking, the Platform will:
   (a) attempt to rebook the Tourist with another available Guide at no extra cost where feasible;
   (b) issue a full refund to the Tourist where rebooking is not feasible;
   (c) record the cancellation in the Guide's reliability metrics, which may affect Platform ranking but does not result in monetary penalty save where caused by Guide misconduct repeatedly impacting Tourists. [COUNSEL REVIEW — careful to avoid punitive deductions that could look like employment discipline.]

7.4 **No-show.**
   (a) **Tourist no-show:** The Guide retains the Service Fee per the Guide's selected cancellation policy; the Platform retains its Information Service Fee.
   (b) **Guide no-show:** The Tourist receives a full refund and a goodwill remediation credit (at the Platform's discretion); the Guide receives nothing and the Platform may take reliability-based delisting action under Article 10.

7.5 **Force majeure.** Cancellations caused by force majeure events (natural disaster, government action, public health emergency, transportation system failure) trigger the Platform's force-majeure protocol, under which neither Party is penalized but the Tourist is refunded.

## ARTICLE 9. DATA HANDLING

9.1 **Platform Privacy Policy applies.** The Platform's processing of personal information of Tourists, Guides, and third parties is governed by the Platform's Privacy Policy, as updated from time to time. The Guide acknowledges having reviewed, and consents to, the current version.

9.2 **Guide as personal-information handler.** When the Guide receives Tourist personal information (name, contact, itinerary details, dietary needs, etc.) through the Platform, the Guide acts as an **independent personal-information handler** (个人信息处理者) under PIPL Article 73 with respect to that information for purposes of delivering the tour. The Guide shall:

   (b) not retain Tourist personal information longer than necessary, and in any case delete it within 90 days after Completion unless legally required to retain longer;

9.5 **No marketing use without consent.** The Platform shall not use Guide personal information for marketing of third-party products to the Guide without separate, opt-in consent.

## ARTICLE 10. TERM AND TERMINATION

10.1 **Term.** This Agreement commences on the date the Guide accepts it electronically at onboarding and continues until terminated under this Article.

10.2 **Termination for convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any or no reason on **thirty (30) days' prior written notice** to the other Party. Notice may be given in-app or by email to the registered address.

10.3 **Immediate termination by Platform.** The Platform may suspend or terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:
   (a) the Guide License is suspended, revoked, expired, or otherwise invalidated;
   (b) the Guide is convicted of, or formally charged with, an offense that creates risk to Tourist safety;
   (c) the Guide commits material breach of Article 3 (Guide Obligations) and fails to cure within 7 days of notice (where cure is possible);
   (d) the Guide engages in fraud, including off-platform-payment circumvention under Article 5.6;
   (e) the Guide makes repeated misrepresentations in profile content;
   (f) continued performance would expose the Platform to material legal or regulatory risk.

10.4 **Immediate termination by Guide.** The Guide may terminate immediately upon written notice if:
   (a) the Platform materially breaches Article 4 or Article 5 (e.g., fails to remit Service Fees due) and does not cure within 7 days of notice;
   (b) the Platform becomes insolvent or ceases operations;
   (c) continued performance would expose the Guide to material legal or regulatory risk.

10.5 **Effect of termination.**
   (a) **Pending Bookings:** Bookings already confirmed before the termination notice shall, at the Guide's option, be honored (with the Platform processing payment as normal) or cancelled per the Guide's cancellation policy. The Platform will not punish a Guide for honoring pending Bookings during the notice period.
   (b) **Outstanding payments:** The Platform shall remit all undisputed amounts due to the Guide within 15 business days of the final Completed Service.
   (c) **Data:** The Platform's retention of Guide data post-termination is governed by the Privacy Policy.
   (d) **Surviving clauses:** Articles 5.5 (refund reversals), 6 (IP), 8 (liability), 9 (data), 11 (dispute), 12 (governing law) and all accrued rights and obligations survive.

10.6 **No severance, no continuation payments.** Because this is not an employment relationship, termination — whether by either Party, with or without cause — gives rise to **no severance, no economic compensation, and no notice-period compensation** beyond Service Fees already earned for Completed Services.

> **Operational note for Product / Operations:** Drafting alone does not establish independent-contractor status. **Day-to-day operational behavior must match the contract.** If Operations imposes performance-management practices, sets fixed schedules, requires uniforms, or treats reliability metrics as employee discipline, the agreement's protections will not survive scrutiny. > **Residual risk flag:** Even with all 12 safeguards above, PRC labor arbitration tribunals retain broad fact-finding discretion. Recent gig-economy cases (e.g., food delivery, ride-hail) have produced inconsistent outcomes. **A residual misclassification risk remains and cannot be drafted to zero.** This is a material risk to escalate to the Architect (system design implications: avoid features that look like employment supervision) and to Business (financial reserve for potential reclassification liability).
